# Portfolio Audit Checklist

Use this to review an existing product design portfolio.

## First 20 Seconds

- The target role is obvious.
- The candidate's strongest skill signal is visible.
- The homepage shows selected work, not a generic gallery.
- The case studies have enough metadata to decide what to open.
- Contact or next step is easy to find.

## Good Markers

- Clear role positioning.
- 2-4 relevant case studies.
- Case studies show context, challenge, process, and result.
- Candidate contribution is explicit.
- Work is ordered by hiring relevance, not chronology.
- Visual craft supports the story.
- Case studies are scannable before they are detailed.
- NDA constraints are handled honestly without making the case empty.
- AI-assisted writing still sounds specific and evidence-based.

## Bad Markers

- No clear target role or company type.
- Too many weak projects.
- Case studies start with final UI and skip the problem.
- Process is generic or decorative.
- Candidate role is unclear.
- Metrics or impact claims are vague or unverifiable.
- About section is long but does not improve hiring fit.
- Contact path is buried.
- Screens look polished but the decision story is absent.
- Student projects are framed as taste exercises instead of problem-solving work.

## Audit Output Format

Return:

1. Top hiring risks.
2. What to keep.
3. What to remove or demote.
4. Homepage rewrite.
5. Case-study ordering.
6. Case-study rewrite notes.
7. Missing evidence to request from the user.
8. Next 3 actions.
