## C2P-R Story

### Context

Explain the product, audience, business setting, team, timeline, and candidate role. Keep this short enough that a reviewer can understand the project without background research.

### Challenge

State the core user or business problem. Include stakes, constraints, and why this project mattered.

Useful prompts:

- What was broken or missing?
- Who was affected?
- What would happen if the team did nothing?
- What constraints shaped the work?

### Process

Show decision quality, not every activity. Include the few steps that changed the direction of the work.

Include:

- Research or discovery method.
- Key insight.
- Options considered.
- Tradeoffs.
- Collaboration.
- Iterations.
- Validation or feedback.
- Design details that prove craft.

Avoid:

- Long generic design-process timelines.
- Screens without explanation.
- Claiming teamwork without explaining the candidate contribution.
- Final UI appearing before the problem is understood.

### Result

Show what changed.

Use available proof:

- Product shipped, launched, piloted, or handed off.
- Metric movement, even directional or anonymized.
- User feedback.
- Business result.
- Decision unlocked.
- Learning and next step.

If no measurable result exists, state the strongest honest outcome and what would be measured next.

## Rewrite Pattern

Weak:

`I redesigned the dashboard to make it cleaner.`

Stronger:

`I redesigned the dashboard around the 3 decisions operations managers made daily, reducing the scan path and making exceptions visible before routine data.`

Weak:

`I did research, wireframes, UI, and testing.`

Stronger:

`I led 6 user calls, found that failed handoffs caused most rework, tested 2 workflow models, and shipped the version that kept ownership visible across teams.`
